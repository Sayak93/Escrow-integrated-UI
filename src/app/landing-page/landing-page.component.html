What is Escrow?

Escrow refers to a third-party
service that's usually mandatory in
a home purchase. When a buyer
and seller initially arrive at a
purchase agreement, they select a
neutral third party to act as the
escrow agent. The escrow agent
collects what is known as "earnest
money" from the buyer: a deposit
that is equal to a small percentage
of the sale price. In exchange, the
seller takes the property off the
market. Until the final exchange is
completed, both the buyer's
deposit and the seller's property
are said to be in escrow.
